		Network Access layer(1): This is the physical connection and data formating happen. 
Sending an Ethernet or Wi-Fi packet are examples of layer 1 concerns. 

		Internet layer(2): This layer deals with the concerns of addressing packets and routing them over multiple interconnection networks. 
It's at this layer tat an IP address is defined. 

		Host-to-Hots layer(3): The host-to-host layer provides two protocals, TCP and UDP. 
These protocols address concerns such as data order, data segmentation, network congestion, and error correction. 

		Process/Application layer(4): The process/application layer iws where protocols such as HTTP, SMTP, and FTP are implemented. 
Most of the programs could be considered to take place on this layer consuming functionality provided by you operating system's implementation of the lower layers.
